## Design Context

### Users
Individual developers who run multiple AI coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, Gemini, etc.) on their local machine. They accumulate dozens of extensions — skills, MCP servers, plugins, hooks — across agents with no unified view of what's installed, where, or whether it's safe. They're technical but time-constrained; they want clarity, not complexity.

### Brand Personality
**Friendly, Clear, Approachable.** HarnessKit is the calm, competent companion that organizes what would otherwise be scattered and opaque. It doesn't lecture about security — it shows you the picture and lets you decide. Think Arc browser's warmth, not CrowdStrike's alarm bells.

### Emotional Goals
- **Confidence**: "I know exactly what's installed and it's all safe."
- **Control**: "I have complete mastery over my extensions."
- Security information should feel empowering, not anxiety-inducing.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Clean, warm, subtly editorial. Closer to a well-designed utility than a dashboard.
- **References**: Linear (clarity), Raycast (precision), Arc (approachability)
- **Anti-references**:
  - NOT enterprise security dashboards (CrowdStrike, Splunk) — avoid fear-driven UI, red alert overload, visual density
  - NOT generic admin panels (Bootstrap/Material defaults) — avoid looking like a CRUD template
  - NOT flashy dev tools — avoid style over substance, excessive motion, distraction
- **Theme**: Frosted glass desktop app aesthetic with 3 distinct themes. The frosted glass sidebar with translucent edges is a signature element — preserve and refine it.
- **Typography**: Distinctive per-theme font stacks (Geist, Outfit, Inter) — each theme has its own personality.

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't alarm.** Present security information as clear status, not warnings. A trust score of 72 is a fact, not a threat.
2. **One glance, full picture.** Every page should communicate its core message within 2 seconds. Hierarchy over density.
3. **Warmth in precision.** Technical information delivered with approachable design. No cold, clinical interfaces.
4. **Respect the developer.** No hand-holding, no redundant explanations. Developers are smart — give them the data and get out of the way.
5. **Signature glass.** The frosted glass layout is the visual identity. Refine it, don't dilute it.
